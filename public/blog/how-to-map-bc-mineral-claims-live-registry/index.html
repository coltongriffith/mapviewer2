What Is BC Mineral Titles Online (MTO) — and Why This Is Different

BC Mineral Titles Online (MTO) is the BC government's authoritative registry of all active mineral, placer, and coal tenure in the province. It's operated by the BC Ministry of Energy and Climate Solutions under the Mineral Tenure Act. MTO lets you search by owner name, claim number, map sheet, and other criteria — but its output is a table of results and a basic map viewer. You cannot export a presentation-quality map from MTO.

Exploration Maps queries the exact same underlying data — BC's public Web Feature Service (WFS) at openmaps.gov.bc.ca, dataset WHSE_MINERAL_TENURE.MTA_ACQUIRED_TENURE_SVW — but wraps it in a design and export tool. The data is identical; the output is a professional map.

The Three Search Modes — and How Auto-Detection Works

The BC Registry Search panel has three tabs. You can switch between them manually, or just start typing — the tool auto-detects which mode is appropriate based on what you enter:

Mode 1

Company Name

Searches the OWNER_NAME field. Matches partial names — "Teck" returns Teck Resources, Teck Coal Ltd., etc. Default mode for text input.

Mode 2 · Auto-detect

Claim # (TAG_NUMBER)

Type any string of digits — e.g. 1034521 — and the tool auto-switches to this mode. Exact match on the claim's TAG_NUMBER. Returns one specific claim.

Mode 3 · Auto-detect

NTS Map Sheet

Type an NTS code — e.g. 093A or 082F056 — and the tool auto-switches. Returns all active claims on that sheet regardless of holder.

Auto-detection rule: All digits → Claim # mode. Pattern like 093A (three digits + letter) → Map Sheet mode. Everything else → Company mode. Click any tab to override manually.

Searching by Claim Number (TAG_NUMBER)

Every BC mineral claim has a unique TAG_NUMBER — a numeric identifier assigned by the Mineral Titles Branch when the claim is recorded. You'll see TAG_NUMBERs referenced in news releases ("the company holds Claim 1034521, comprising 329 hectares..."), NI 43-101 technical reports, and MTO title searches.

To find a specific claim: switch to the Claim # tab, enter the TAG_NUMBER, and press Search. The exact claim polygon loads with its owner name, tenure type, area, and expiry date visible. You can also just start typing digits — the tool auto-detects and switches to Claim # mode automatically.

Searching by NTS Map Sheet

BC's mineral tenure system uses the National Topographic System (NTS) grid as its spatial reference. Each 1:50,000 map sheet covers roughly 1,800 km². Cell claims are assigned an NTS location code (MAP_UNIT_NO) identifying their exact sheet and cell within it.

Map Sheet search returns all active claims on a given sheet, regardless of holder. This is the most useful mode for answering the question "who has staked in this area?" — for competitive intelligence, new staking strategy, or identifying potential neighbours for a joint venture.

Uploading a Shapefile Instead of Searching

If you've already downloaded claims data from MTO, the BC Data Catalogue (catalogue.data.gov.bc.ca), or another GIS source, use the Upload File path in the Add Claims dialog. Accepted formats:

  • Shapefile .zip — all components (.shp, .dbf, .prj, .shx) zipped together, or all four files dragged together without zipping
  • GeoJSON / JSON — direct drop from QGIS, ESRI, or any GIS export
  • KML / KMZ — from Google Earth Pro or ArcGIS Online
BC Albers auto-reprojection: Shapefiles downloaded from the BC government (DataBC, MTO) typically use BC Albers (EPSG:3005) — a projected coordinate system with easting values around 1,000,000 m. If the tool detects coordinates that can't be valid WGS84 (|x| > 180 or |y| > 90), it automatically reprojects from EPSG:3005 to WGS84 using proj4 before adding the layer. No QGIS reprojection step needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I search for BC mineral claims by company name?
Open Exploration Maps and click Search BC Registry Claims. Make sure the Company tab is selected (it's the default), type the company name, and press Enter. Results come from BC's live MTO database — no login needed. The tool searches the OWNER_NAME field and supports partial name matching. Alternatively, use BC's official Mineral Titles Online portal directly, but note it doesn't produce exportable maps.
How do I find out who owns a mineral claim in BC?
Every BC mineral claim has a registered owner (OWNER_NAME) in the Mineral Titles Online system. If you have the claim's TAG_NUMBER, switch to the Claim # tab in Exploration Maps and enter it — the result shows the owner name, tenure type, hectares, and expiry date. Without the TAG_NUMBER, use BC's MTO title search and search by map location or claim name.
Can I download BC mineral claims as a shapefile?
Yes. The complete BC mineral claims dataset is available for free download from the BC Data Catalogue (dataset: WHSE_MINERAL_TENURE.MTA_ACQUIRED_TENURE_SVW). The download uses BC Albers (EPSG:3005). If you import this shapefile into Exploration Maps, coordinates are auto-reprojected to WGS84. For most mapping use cases, the live search in Exploration Maps is faster than downloading the full dataset.
What is the difference between Exploration Maps and BC Mineral Titles Online (MTO)?
MTO is BC's official tenure management and search portal — it lets you apply for claims, renew titles, and search the registry. It has a basic map viewer but cannot produce exportable maps for presentations or reports. Exploration Maps queries the same live WFS data as MTO but is designed for creating export-ready professional maps. Use MTO to manage your tenure; use Exploration Maps to build maps from that data.
How current is the BC mineral claims data?
The data is live — each search queries BC's WFS directly. Newly granted claims typically appear within 24–48 hours of registration in MTO. Expired claims (past their GOOD_TO_DATE) are automatically excluded from results. The last-updated timestamp for the WFS dataset is visible in the Open Government Portal metadata.
Can I add claims from multiple companies to one map?
Yes. Each search creates a separate layer. Search for Company A, add their claims (Layer 1, blue), then search for Company B and add theirs (Layer 2, orange). Both appear in the legend with independent controls for colour, opacity, and visibility. This is the fastest way to build a competitive tenure landscape map.
Does the company hold claims under a subsidiary name?
Mineral claims are registered under the exact legal entity that holds the title — often a subsidiary, not the parent company. If a search for "Teck" returns fewer results than expected, try "Teck Coal" or "Highland Valley Copper Partnership." You can also search by NTS map sheet to find all claims in the geographic area regardless of legal holder name — then look at the OWNER_NAME field in the results to identify related entities.
How do I make a BC mineral claims map for an NI 43-101 report?
Search for the property's claim block in Exploration Maps, select the relevant claims, and add them to the map. The layer loads with standard blue claims styling. Toggle on the North Arrow and Scale Bar in Design (both required under NI 43-101 guidelines). Set the design template to Technical Report or NI 43-101. Add the Map Date and Project Number in the text fields. Export as PDF at Letter Landscape or A3 size. The exported PDF is vector-quality and suitable for inclusion in a technical report filed under Form 43-101F1.
Is this tool free?
Yes. Searching the BC registry and building a map is completely free with no account required. Watermark-free PNG, SVG, and PDF exports ask for your email once — no subscription, no password, no credit card. The email is stored locally in your browser and not used for marketing without consent.
