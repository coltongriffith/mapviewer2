How to Map BC Mineral Claims from the Live Registry — No Download Required

Published June 8, 2026 · 8 min read

You can now search BC's live Mineral Titles Online (MTO) registry directly inside Exploration Maps — by company name, claim number, or NTS map sheet. Select the claims you want, click Add to map, and your claim boundaries load instantly with automatic styling. The full workflow from search to export takes under five minutes.

British Columbia has over 100,000 active mineral claims held by everyone from major producers like Teck Resources and First Quantum Minerals to junior explorers with a single cell claim. Getting those boundaries onto a professional-looking map used to mean downloading a shapefile from MTO, converting it in QGIS, and manually importing it. That process is gone. Here's exactly how the new BC Registry search works.

The Three Ways to Search

The BC claims search panel gives you three search modes. You can switch between them using the tabs at the top of the search panel, or just start typing — the tool auto-detects which mode applies based on what you enter.

Tab 1

Company Name

Search by the registered holder name. Works for any company in BC's mineral tenure registry. Auto-selected by default.

Tab 2

Claim #

Enter a specific TAG_NUMBER (e.g. 1034521). Returns that exact claim. Auto-detected when you type digits only.

Tab 3

Map Sheet

Enter an NTS map sheet code (e.g. 093A, 082F). Returns all active claims on that sheet. Auto-detected by the NTS pattern.

Auto-detection: If you type 1034521 (all digits), the tool auto-switches to Claim # mode. If you type 093A (an NTS code), it switches to Map Sheet. For anything else, it stays on Company name. You can always override by clicking a tab manually.

Full Workflow: Teck Resources Claims in BC

Teck Resources is Canada's largest diversified mining company, headquartered in Vancouver. They hold active mineral claims across multiple regions of BC — including the Highland Valley area (home to one of the world's largest open-pit copper mines), the East Kootenay, and parts of the Golden Triangle in the northwest. Here's the complete workflow using Teck as the example.

  1. Open the Add Claims tool

    From the landing page, click the Search BC Registry Claims button. If you're already in the editor, click + Add BC Claims in the left sidebar. A modal opens with two options: Search BC Registry and Upload File. Click Search BC Registry.

  2. Type "Teck" and search

    The search panel opens with three mode tabs at the top. The Company tab is active by default. Type Teck (or "Teck Resources") in the search field and press Enter or click Search. The tool queries BC's live WFS endpoint at openmaps.gov.bc.ca — the same data source powering the official MTO viewer.

  5. Claims load as a styled layer — map fits automatically

    Click Add 184 claims to map. The modal closes, the claim polygons appear on the map as a new layer named "Teck Resources — Highland Valley Area Claims," and the map fits to the claim boundaries. The layer is automatically styled with a blue stroke and semi-transparent blue fill — the conventional claims styling used in NI 43-101 technical reports.

    The layer appears in the Layers panel in the sidebar. You can rename it, change the fill and stroke colour, adjust opacity, and toggle it on or off in the legend independently of other layers.

  6. Build the map design around your claims

    With the claims layer on the map, building a finished investor presentation or technical figure takes a few more clicks:

    • Basemap: Choose Satellite for regional context, or Light for a clean technical report look.
    • Reference overlays: Turn on Roads & Settlements so viewers can orient themselves. Reduce opacity to 50% so roads don't compete with your claim boundaries.
    • Title: Click the title block and enter your project name — e.g. "Highland Valley Copper — Mineral Claims." Add the map date and project number.
    • Distance ring: If you want to show proximity to infrastructure, click the ring tool and draw a 10 km or 25 km radius from a key point (mine site, highway, power line).
    • Callouts: Click the annotation tool to add a text label pointing to a specific claim block or geographic feature.
    • North arrow & scale bar: Toggle both on in the Design section — required for NI 43-101 maps.
  7. Export the finished map

    Click PNG for PowerPoint slides, news releases, and web use. Click PDF for print-quality technical reports and regulatory filings. Click SVG to get an editable vector file you can bring into Illustrator or Inkscape for final production work.

    Watermark-free exports prompt for your email — no password, no subscription, no credit card. The email is saved locally so you're not asked again.

Tip for multi-company comparisons: Search for Teck, add their claims, then search for an adjacent junior explorer and add their claims as a second layer in a different colour. This creates a competitive landscape map showing claim positions relative to each other — a common slide in project presentation decks.

What Data Does the Search Return?

Every result comes directly from the BC government's public WFS endpoint (WHSE_MINERAL_TENURE.MTA_ACQUIRED_TENURE_SVW). This is the same dataset behind BC's official MTO viewer. For each claim, you get:

Searching by Claim Number or Map Sheet

Claim number search

If you have a specific claim number from a news release, technical report, or MTO portal, switch to the Claim # tab and enter it directly. You can also just type the number — if it's all digits, the tool auto-detects and switches to claim number mode. This is useful for quickly pulling up a specific claim referenced in an NI 43-101 report or a competitor's news release.

Map sheet search

BC's mineral claims system uses the National Topographic System (NTS) grid. Every claim is assigned to a 1:50,000 or 1:250,000 map sheet. If you're working in a specific area — say the Toodoggone district (104H) or the Cariboo gold belt (093A) — switch to Map Sheet and enter the sheet code. The search returns all active claims on that sheet, regardless of who holds them, giving you a full picture of the competitive claim landscape in that area.

NTS code format: A primary sheet is three digits + letter, e.g. 093A or 082F. You can also enter a more specific subsection (e.g. 093A056) to get a tighter area. If you're not sure of the sheet code for an area, look it up at maps.canada.ca or use the MTO portal to find the map sheet number for a specific coordinate.

Uploading a Shapefile Instead

The live registry search is fastest for finding claims by name, number, or area. But if you've already downloaded claims data from MTO, Natural Earth, or another source, the Upload File path in the Add Claims dialog accepts:

  • .zip shapefile — all four components (.shp, .dbf, .prj, .shx) zipped together, or dropped individually
  • GeoJSON / JSON
  • KML / KMZ

If the shapefile uses BC Albers projection (EPSG:3005 — the default CRS for BC government shapefiles), the tool automatically detects the projected coordinates and reprojects them to WGS84 before adding them to the map. No QGIS step needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Exploration Maps connect directly to BC Mineral Titles Online?
Yes. The search queries BC's public WFS endpoint at openmaps.gov.bc.ca in real time — the same data source that powers the official MTO viewer. Active mineral claims, cell claims, and legacy licences are all included. Expired claims are not returned. No download or conversion step is needed.
Can I search for claims from the Yukon or Ontario in the same tool?
The current BC Registry search is specific to British Columbia's provincial WFS. Claims from other jurisdictions need to be imported as shapefiles or GeoJSON — which you can get from each province's or territory's mineral lands portal (e.g. Ontario's MLAS, Yukon's ADAM, Alberta's Mineral Rights Viewer).
How current is the BC mineral claims data?
It's live — each search queries the WFS directly. Newly granted claims typically appear within 24–48 hours of registration. Expired claims are excluded from results automatically based on the GOOD_TO_DATE field.
Can I add claims from multiple companies to one map?
Yes. Each search adds a separate layer. Search for Teck, add their claims (Layer 1, blue), then search for a junior explorer and add theirs (Layer 2, red). Both layers appear in the legend with independent colour controls. This is the fastest way to build a competitive landscape map.
What if the company holds claims under a subsidiary name?
Mineral claims in BC are registered under the legal entity name that holds the mineral title, which is often a subsidiary, not the parent company. If a search for "Teck" doesn't return what you expect, try searching for specific operating subsidiaries like "Teck Coal" or "Highland Valley Copper." You can also use Map Sheet search to find all claims in the geographic area regardless of holder.
Is this feature free?
Yes. Searching the BC registry and adding claims to a map is completely free — no account required. Watermark-free exports (PNG, SVG, PDF) ask for your email address. No subscription or password is needed.
Can I style the claims layer after importing?
Yes. Click the layer in the Layers panel to expand its style controls. You can change the fill colour, stroke colour, opacity, and stroke weight. You can also rename the layer — the name appears in the legend. If you have multiple claim layers, give each a descriptive name like "Company A — Option Claims" so the legend reads clearly on the exported map.
